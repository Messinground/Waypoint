In cats, calico coats are associated with the X chromosome, thus they normally only appear in females.

Male calicos, however, are not unheard of. This can be caused by chimerism or mosaicism - where the cat is born with cells containing both male and female genetics.

This can also be caused by Klinefelter syndrome, where the cat is born with XXY chromosomes.

Calico cats are identical to tortoiseshell in every way except that their undercoat is white, wherease tortoiseshells' undercoats are black. Thus there have also been Tortoiseshell Gynanders.

In German and Japanese folklore, calico's are considered to be good luck.

This of course comes in contrast to black cats, which are often imagined to be bad luck.

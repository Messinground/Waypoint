Wrestling needs to be more absurd

December 17, 2025

A while back Charlie (Penguin0 on YouTube) talked about a wrestling match he saw where Jesus was one of the competitors. He was using telekinesis and everyone was playing it up like it was real. I bet that would have been a HILARIOUS show to watch.

This sent me down the rabbit hole. What other wrestling matches just threw plausibility out the window in favor of entertainment? My dad showed me a match between two invisible wrestlers where the only way to follow what was happening was by watching the ref's reaction, as he wore "invisibility glasses."

It got me thinking about how wrestling is planned out ahead of time. It takes real athleticism, but nevertheless the majority of hits are purposely choreographed to be safer. Sometimes there's never any real contact at all. And impressive as the athleticism is, it's hard for people like me to get invested when the artificiality of the fights are so evident. Every movie requires suspension of disbelief, but wrestling requires a little too much, I think.

Which begs the question: why bother with realism at all? Throw it out the window! Make absurdity like telekinesis and invisibility regularly recurring themes. In WWE the closest thing I've seen is how they'll sometimes bring puppets into fights and treat them like real living people/animals. They could REALLY kick it up a few notches.

Proof of concept: you could easily introduce a character who's just a middle-aged guy that keeps accidentally walking into the wrong room. He wears business casual because he just came from the office. Every character has a theme song, so this is his. How can such a character compete? Accidently. He spends every match running away and dodging hits, then in loony-toons fashion, somehow ends up winning every fight until every wrestler is terrified at the sight of him.

More characters likeÂ that and you'll have a new dedicated viewer.
